# SME Review Pack — Driving Me Nuts
## Small-Business Accuracy Gate (P1 Exit)

---

## Project Brief (Non-Gamer Context)

**Driving Me Nuts** is a pixel-art game teaching real small-business economics through the daily operations of a roasted-peanut food truck. Players make decisions about pricing, inventory, permits, location strategy, and cash timing — all grounded in actual food-truck economics. **The core teaching rule is "simplified but never wrong":** game numbers are stylized derivatives of real-world figures, but they stay plausible and internally consistent. The goal is for players to learn *why* small businesses fail or thrive; the game is not aspirational fantasy.

**Your job:** verify that every factual claim about real-world food-truck operations, permit costs, ingredient pricing, and small-business mechanics is accurate enough for an educator to trust. This is a 2–4 hour focused review; you are NOT reviewing game balance, fun, or design choices.

---

## Reviewer Instructions

**What to check:**
- Real-world plausibility of every cost figure (permits, ingredients, fuel, labor).
- Whether the economics chain together logically (e.g., if COGS is 30% of price, can a small operator survive on that margin?).
- Whether claimed permit costs, renewal cycles, and location rules match actual jurisdictions (within reason; we accept "varies by location").
- Whether the cash-flow examples add up and reflect real cash-flow problems.
- Whether seasonality patterns match real food-truck experience.

**What NOT to review:**
- Game balance or difficulty (fun is not your domain).
- Whether the UI is good or the pixel art is pretty.
- Whether the prestige loop is engaging or the legume gag lands.
- Pedagogical fit (is it suitable for your target students?) — **unless** you spot a business-accuracy issue that affects teaching.

**How to mark findings:**

| Finding Type | Mark | Example |
|---|---|---|
| **WRONG** | ❌ WRONG | "Permits cost $500; actually they're $1,200 in my area and non-negotiable." |
| **MISLEADING-SIMPLIFICATION** | ⚠️ MISLEADING | "The game says COGS is $0.42 but omits packaging; real total is $0.65." |
| **OK** | ✅ OK | "Permit cost of $500 is simplified but in the right ballpark." |
| **OK-BUT-ADD-CAVEAT** | ✅ +CAVEAT | "$500 permit is plausible; add a tooltip: 'varies by jurisdiction.'" |

## Open Questions (From BUSINESS_CURRICULUM.md §11)

Please answer these as part of your review:

1. **Permit Realism (Q1 in curriculum):** Is $500 for combined health + mobile-vendor permit plausible in your jurisdiction or target region? Should the game vary by difficulty (tutorial = simplified, hard mode = real numbers)?

2. **Seasonality Granularity (Q2):** Real food trucks adjust for specific events within seasons (e.g., "Pumpkin Fest Oct 15–20, +50% traffic"). Should the game model individual events or stick to seasonal bands?

3. **Debt & Credit (Q3):** Should the game allow credit cards or formal loans? Or keep it simple: cash-only, with an optional "emergency loan" (high interest) for late-game learning?

4. **Allergy Mechanic Depth (Q4):** Beyond NPC allergy mentions, should the game require periodic food-safety certifications, or is "Health Permit renewal" gate sufficient?

5. **Prestige Loop Design (Q5):** On NG+, should fixed costs reset (new truck, new permits) or carry over (you "bought" it, keep the asset)? This changes entire year-2 strategy.

6. **Educator Mode (Q6):** Should there be a "curriculum mode" that locks certain UI elements (no prestige, simplified permits) for school use? Or keep one coherent design?

7. **Narrative Depth (Q7):** Should the legume-lecture NPC have a story arc (e.g., become a friend, teach about botany), or stay a recurring gag?
