# Legume Lore: The Running Gag Collectible Set

**Driving Me Nuts** — the canonical dialogue variants for the Legume Gag mechanic.

Canon premise: Customers inform the owner that "peanuts aren't nuts, they're legumes," triggering the owner's weary or clever response. Each variant is indexed, tiered (early/mid/late game), tagged with delivery tone, and grounded in one specific joke angle.

**Total count: 43 variants** (QUALITY > COUNT per risk register; no padding).

---

**Game-time gate note (W5):** In-game tier gates are compressed vs the narrative day ranges
above. At 14-minute real days nobody reaches "Day 21" soon, so gates are: mid=day 8,
late=day 20. The narrative arc order (early→mid→late) is canon; the absolute day numbers
are tunable. LL-017–020 belong to the LATE narrative section and are gated at day 20+.

## Writing Style Notes (For Future Variants)

**Proven mechanics for new variants:**

1. **Repetition as humor:** Owner quantifies how many times he's heard the fact (847th person, etc.). Builds cumulative joke.
2. **Pivot to superiority:** Don't argue legume-ness; pivot to product quality ("best legumes in three counties").
3. **Preemption:** Owner cuts the customer off before they finish. Shows he's internalized the pattern.
4. **Meta-business commentary:** Owner treats the joke as intentional branding ("I named my business after that exact conversation").
5. **Affection + resignation:** Owner's annoyance is warm, not hostile. He's bonded with customers over the repetition.
6. **Zen acceptance:** Late-game owner has made peace with the absurdity and owns it proudly.
7. **Customer journey recognition:** Owner treats each new customer's realization as a milestone in their education.
8. **Weaponized expertise:** Owner learns enough botany/nutrition to respond with equal sophistication, then pivots to sales.
9. **Absurdist escalation:** New botanical facts (cashews as berries) prompt owner to expand his world-weariness philosophically.
10. **Marketing flip:** The legume fact, once annoying, becomes the truck's brand identity and a source of owner pride.

**Tone spectrum (tightest to loosest):**
- Deadpan (no emotion, pure logic)
- Weary (tired but still functioning)
- Sarcastic (truth wrapped in false negation)
- Smug (confident, slightly superior, but not hostile)
- Warm (affectionate, bonded)
- Zen (at peace with absurdity)
- Kid-like exasperation (playful, slightly theatrical)
