## Driving Me Nuts — Preview

**A pixel-art RPG + idle game about running a roasted-peanut food truck.**

You inherit a rusty truck, $50 cash, and 20 lbs of raw peanuts. Your job: roast, price, sell, and grow into a local legend. The catch? Running a real business means **real economics**—COGS, margins, break-even, and the eternal pricing puzzle.

The running gag is canon: customers keep telling you "peanuts aren't nuts, they're legumes." You reply (tired at first, then clever, then proud): *"I know, I know—it's driving me nuts!"*

---

## What's Playable Now (P1)

**The core day cycle:** Load raw peanuts into your roaster. Pick a selling price. Work 6am–8pm. Sell to the Farmers' Market crowd. Watch your profit (or loss) on the end-of-day report card.

Every sale teaches: the cheaper you price, the more you sell—but margin shrinks. The higher you price, fewer sales—but higher per-unit profit. The game shows you the math in real time so you can feel why **pricing is strategy.**

---

## What Makes It Different

- **Idle that respects your time:** No mandatory grind. No countdown timers forcing you to log in daily. No streaks. Play for 10 minutes or 2 hours—there's no FOMO.
- **Real small-business math under the hood:** COGS (Cost of Goods Sold), gross margin, break-even analysis, inventory management. The numbers are plausible, simplified for clarity, and sourced to real-world food-truck benchmarks.
- **A running joke as a game mechanic:** The peanuts-aren't-nuts gag isn't just flavor text. It triggers mechanics (Legume Lore collectibles, gag combos, bonuses). The joke evolves from annoyance into brand identity—just like the owner's journey.

## Accessibility

- **Reading level:** Conversational. Tooltips are optional; core mechanics are visual.
- **Pacing:** No time pressure. Pause and think as long as you want.
- **Colorblind-friendly:** Colors are always paired with text/numbers so you're never reading color alone.

## For Educators

If you're teaching business concepts (pricing, COGS, profit, break-even), this game is designed for classrooms. An educator's guide is available on request—it maps game mechanics to business curriculum and includes a 45-minute lesson plan.

The game collects zero data. It's fully offline after the initial load. No accounts. No leaderboards. No analytics.
